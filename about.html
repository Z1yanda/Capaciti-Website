About CAPACITI

CAPACITI, a non-profit, transcends traditional digital training by inspiring and mentoring individuals to thrive in the digital world.

Our Mission

CAPACITI aims to democratize tech careers and foster inclusive societies in Africa, viewing quality education as essential for a diverse and growing digital economy.

Our Vision

We aim to empower digitally marginalized youth with technical skills and work readiness, focusing on meaningful career opportunities beyond just job acquisition.

More About Us

Established in 2010, CAPACITI, part of the UVU Africa Group, is at the forefront of preparing young individuals for the tech sector. CAPACITI has emerged as one of South Africa's leading digital talent accelerators, annually training and placing approximately 1000 unemployed youth.

CAPACITI History Timeline

1997

Cluster Muster of ICT

A catalytic event bringing civic activists together to envision an inclusive digital society in Cape Town and beyond.

1999

Incorporation of CiTi

Established as a Not-for-Profit Company to build an inclusive digital economy in Cape Town.

2000

CiTi's First Premises

Opened Ovenstone House as its first space, incubating what would become the Bandwidth Barn.

2001

Launch of Bandwidth Barn

Established a co-working space in Lower Long Street, growing into Cape Town's tech hub.

2010

CapaCiTi Launches

Founded to address the tech skills gap by training unemployed South African youth.

2011

First BASA Programme

Launched with UCT to provide unemployed graduates with business analysis training through a postgraduate diploma.

2015

FinTech Cluster & Blockchain Academy

CiTi launches South Africa's first Blockchain Academy and a FinTech cluster to promote financial inclusion.

2016

Expansion & Partnerships

Opened new campuses in Cape Town and Johannesburg; secured $19M Jobs Fund grant; partnered with Thomson Reuters, Naspers, and others.

2017

EdTech & FinTech Academies

Launched Injini (Africa's first EdTech incubator) and FinTech Academy to merge global FinTech trends with local skills.

2018

Major Investment

BCX committed R100M over three years; CiTi launched additional CapaCiTi campuses with $19M in funding support.

2019

Specialized Innovation Labs

Expanded partnerships to grow digital skills and launched innovation labs focused on youth employment.

2022

Launch of BioCiTi

Established a biotech incubator supporting startups across Africa in the biotech sector.

2023

Rebranding & Platform Launch

CapaCiTi rebranded as CAPACITI under UVU Africa and launched a new OpenEdx-based learning platform and mobile app.

2024

Growth & Expansion

Launched a Tech Career Accelerator in Gqeberha, Eastern Cape, and opened a new CiTi office in Kigali, East Africa.
